Science Fair Project: Sugar Crystal Growth

My Science Fair Experiment was: Growing sugar crystals to finding out if they grow better in distilled water or tap water.

Testable Question:

How does the type of liquid affect sugar crystal growth?

Hypothesis:

I hypothesize that if you use distilled water to grow sugar crystals, then the crystals will grow bigger and better because distilled water is more pure and the water must be pure for sugar crystals to grow well.

Materials

You will need: two small cups, two pencils, two pieces of 20cm cotton string, 100ml of distilled water, 100 ml of tap water, 8.8 ml of sugar, a metal bowl, a cup of boiled or hot water, a stove, a 58ml measuring cup and 1 pitcher.

Variables, Constants, & Trials:

The Independent Variable is the type of water. The Dependent Variable is to see how well sugar crystals grow with different types of water used to grow the crystals. The Constant Conditions are: the location, the time given, the amount of water, the length of string, the amount of sugar, the temperature, the cup size, and the number of trials. The number of trials done was three.

Procedure to Follow

First, gather all the materials listed in the materials list. Next, put the metal bowl on the stove and pour the cup of boiled water into the bowl. Then turn the stove on to high. Then, put the pitcher in the metal bowl and pour in the 100ml of tap water in the pitcher. After that, stir 4.4ml of sugar into the 100ml of tap water in the pitcher until the water is saturated. Pour the mixture into a small cup to about halfway. Next, tie one of the pieces of string to the pencil and dangle it in the water, making sure the string doesn't touch the sides of the small cup so the pencils resting on the sides of the cup. Repeat steps one through six but use 100ml distilled water in step three and four. After repeating the steps with distilled water, place a tissue over each cup and put the cups in a location with no vibration. Record observations after letting the crystals grow for one week in the location.
